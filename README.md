2 set clear problem and goal statement

##Problem Statement
“In Pokhara, busy locals, families, and tourists face long in-person bakery queues, unclear fresh item availability and pricing, language barriers, cash-only payments, and lack of support for daily needs or dietary restrictions  wasting time and causing frustration.”

##Goal Statement
“Create a simple, mobile-first bakery app that enables users in Pokhara to browse real-time menus, customize orders, pay securely in under 2 minutes, and subscribe monthly for daily essentials  reducing queues, increasing accessibility, and making bakery experiences more enjoyable and inclusive.”


#3 prioritise feature 
Must-have (Core MVP – launch version)  
- Digital menu display (photos, prices, “fresh today” indicators,    ingredients list, allergen badges)  
- Basic order customization (size, add-ons)  
- Secure payment integration (eSewa / Khalti / COD)  
- Simple cart & checkout flow  
- Real-time item availability updates  

Should-have (Strongly recommended for first release)  
- Nepali / English language toggle  
- Order history & quick reorder  
- Monthly subscription model (daily bundles, transparent billing preview, easy cancel)  
- Lactose-intolerant & basic dietary filters  
- Chat support link (WhatsApp / Facebook Messenger)  

Could-have (Nice-to-have – test in future iterations)  
- Push notifications (stock updates, promotions, order ready)  
- Loyalty points (earn & redeem)  
- “Surprise Me” AI cake recommendation for birthdays  

Won’t-have (MVP scope)
- Full delivery integration  
- Live bake preparation video/stream  
- Advanced AR previews  

##Ethical & Inclusive Design Notes
- Transparent subscription previews and one-tap cancel (no deceptive patterns)  
- Allergen & nutrition transparency promotes equity (especially for tourists and families)  
- Low-data design, large tappable areas, Nepali support for broader accessibility
